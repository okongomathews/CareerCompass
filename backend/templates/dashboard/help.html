Getting Started

CareerCompass Kenya is an AI-powered career guidance platform built specifically for Kenyan students and young professionals. It combines the internationally validated MBTI (Myers–Briggs Type Indicator) personality framework with Kenya-specific labour-market data to match you with the careers most likely to bring you fulfilment and financial stability.

Key features include:

  • A 60-question MBTI psychometric assessment across four cognitive dimensions
  • Personalised career recommendations ranked by personality fit, academic background, and market demand
  • An AI Career Coach powered by large-language-model technology
  • Learning-path guides and subject recommendations for each career
  • Live salary and market-trend data for the Kenyan job market

Registration takes less than two minutes:

  • Click Register on the login page or go to /users/register/
  • Enter your first name, last name, username, email address, and a secure password
  • Select your account type — Student, Teacher, or Parent/Guardian
  • Submit the form. You will be logged in automatically and a welcome email will be sent to the address you provided

If you already have an account, go to Sign In.

The platform supports three user types:

  • Students — the primary users. Take the personality assessment, receive career matches, and chat with the AI coach to plan your future.
  • Teachers / Guidance Counsellors — monitor student progress and support career conversations in the classroom.
  • Parents / Guardians — stay informed about career options and provide contextual support to their children.

Administrators have a separate analytics dashboard for school-wide or system-wide reporting.

Yes. All core features — the MBTI assessment, career recommendations, AI coach, learning paths, and market-trend explorer — are completely free for registered users. There are no hidden fees or premium tiers at this time.

CareerCompass Kenya works on any modern browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari. The interface is fully responsive and works well on smartphones, tablets, and desktops. For the best experience we recommend Chrome or Edge on a screen wider than 360 px.

Personality Assessment

MBTI stands for Myers–Briggs Type Indicator — a widely used psychometric framework that describes how people perceive the world and make decisions. It measures four cognitive dimensions:

  • E/I — Extraversion vs Introversion: where you direct your energy (outward or inward)
  • S/N — Sensing vs Intuition: how you gather information (concrete facts vs patterns and possibilities)
  • T/F — Thinking vs Feeling: how you make decisions (logic vs values)
  • J/P — Judging vs Perceiving: how you approach the external world (structured vs flexible)

The combination of your preferences in each dimension produces one of 16 personality types (e.g. INTJ, ENFP, ISTJ). CareerCompass uses your type — and the precise percentage scores on each dimension — to match you with careers that align with your natural cognitive style.

The assessment contains 60 questions (15 per dimension). Most users complete it in 15–20 minutes. Your progress is saved automatically after every answer, so you can pause mid-way and resume from where you left off — even if you close your browser.

There are no right or wrong answers. All 16 personality types are equally valid and each maps to rewarding career paths. For the most accurate results:

  • Answer based on your natural tendencies, not what you think is expected of you
  • Go with your first instinct — prolonged deliberation tends to reduce accuracy
  • Think about how you behave most of the time across different life contexts, not just school
  • Avoid answering based on a specific role you currently play (e.g. "class prefect mode")

Yes, you can retake the assessment at any time by clicking the Retake button on your Results page or by navigating to Personality Test in the sidebar.

Important: starting a new session will permanently replace your previous results and regenerate your career recommendations. If you are happy with your current results, view them first at Assessment Results before retaking.

Each dimension (e.g. E/I) shows you a bar split between your two poles — e.g. 60% Extraversion / 40% Introversion. A score closer to 100% on one side means your preference is very clear; a score near 50/50 means you are borderline between the two, which is entirely normal and simply means you are flexible in that dimension.

The overall confidence percentage is a composite measure of how consistently you answered across all four dimensions. A higher value means your type prediction is more reliable. Values above 70% are considered strong.

Your progress is automatically saved after every answer you submit. If you close your browser or lose your internet connection, simply return to Personality Test. The system will detect your incomplete session and resume from where you left off.

Career Recommendations

Each recommendation is computed from a weighted blend of four scores, plus a small additive grade-level adjustment:

  • Personality match (55%) — how closely your MBTI type and dimension percentages align with the cognitive demands of each career; this is the primary driver of your score
  • Academic alignment (30%) — how well your declared subjects and learning style map to the knowledge base required
  • Learning style (10%) — how your preferred way of learning fits the career's typical training path
  • Interest alignment (5%) — overlap between your stated interests and the career's field

Market demand and salary potential are shown on every career page for context, but are deliberately not factored into your personal match score — this keeps your results reflecting who you are rather than just which careers happen to be in demand right now.

Careers scoring 70% or above overall are classified as High Match; 50–69% as Good Match; below 50% as Explore.

Yes. On the My Careers page there is a Regenerate Recommendations button. You may want to do this after:

  • Updating your academic subjects or learning style in your profile
  • Retaking the personality assessment
  • Significant time has passed and market conditions may have shifted

Yes. Use Explore Careers in the sidebar to browse the complete career catalogue. You can filter by category, education level, and salary range, and search by name. Each career page shows a detailed profile including required subjects, personality compatibility, salary data, and a learning path.

Open any career's detail page and click the Compare button, then choose a second career. The comparison view renders both careers side-by-side across personality fit, salary, education requirements, market growth, and required skills — making it easy to weigh trade-offs.

Every career detail page includes a Learning Path — a step-by-step roadmap that shows you exactly which secondary school subjects to prioritise, which university courses or diplomas are most relevant, and which certifications, internships, and skills to build at each stage of your career journey. Click View Learning Path on any career detail page to access it.

Salary ranges are sourced from the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) earnings surveys and supplemented with data from major Kenyan job boards. Market-demand scores are computed from job-posting trends, government sector-growth projections, and World Bank Kenya economic reports. Data is reviewed and updated periodically. You can explore market trends for any career on the Market Trends page.

AI Career Coach

The AI Career Coach is a conversational assistant trained on career guidance principles and Kenya-specific career data. It can help you:

  • Understand what a specific career involves day-to-day
  • Explore your strengths and how they relate to career options
  • Draft a personalised career development plan
  • Identify skill gaps and suggest resources to close them
  • Prepare for university applications or job interviews
  • Think through difficult decisions (e.g. Science vs Arts pathway)
  • Get motivation and accountability support

Access it via AI Coach Chat in the sidebar.

Yes. Every conversation is stored securely in your account under Conversations. You can return to any previous session to review advice, continue the discussion, or start a new topic in a fresh conversation. Conversations are private and visible only to you.

A Coaching Plan is an AI-generated, structured roadmap created specifically for your personality type, target career, and academic situation. It breaks down your journey into timed phases with concrete actions, milestones, and resources. Visit Coaching Plan to generate or review yours.

The AI coach is a decision-support tool, not a substitute for qualified human counselling. While it draws on validated career data, it can occasionally make errors or produce generic responses. We recommend:

  • Cross-checking important information with your school's guidance counsellor or a careers professional
  • Asking follow-up questions to get more specific guidance
  • Verifying salary, university entry requirements, and labour market claims from official Kenyan sources (KNBS, KUCCPS, etc.)

The Learning Resources section (in the AI Coach menu) curates books, online courses, videos, and links relevant to your personality type and target careers. Resources are organised by career category and skill level. You can also ask the AI coach directly to recommend resources for any specific topic.

Account & Profile

Go to My Profile from the sidebar or the top-right user menu. From there click Edit Profile to update your name, school, year of study, subjects, and learning-style preference. Keeping your profile up to date improves the accuracy of your career recommendations.

On the login page click Forgot Password?. You can reset via two methods:

  • OTP (recommended for Kenyan users): enter your email address, receive a one-time code, verify it, and set a new password — all within a few minutes and works perfectly on slow mobile connections
  • Email link: a password-reset link is sent to your registered email address. Click the link within 24 hours to set a new password

If you no longer have access to your registered email address, contact support (see below).

Profile completion tracks the following fields — each contributes to the overall percentage:

  • First and last name
  • School or institution
  • Year / form of study
  • Academic subjects (at least three)
  • Learning style preference
  • Personality assessment completed

A higher completion percentage gives the recommendation engine more data points and produces more accurate, personalised career matches.

Yes. To request account deletion, email support@careercompass.ke with the subject line "Account Deletion Request" from your registered email address. Your account and all associated data will be permanently deleted within 7 business days in accordance with our data retention policy.

Privacy & Data

All personal data is stored on servers located within the East Africa region. We apply the following security measures:

  • Passwords are hashed using Django's PBKDF2-SHA256 algorithm — we never store plain-text passwords
  • All data in transit is encrypted via HTTPS / TLS 1.3
  • Database access is restricted by role-based permissions
  • Regular automated backups with point-in-time recovery

No. CareerCompass Kenya does not sell, share, or rent your personal data to any third party. Your assessment results, conversations, and profile information are used exclusively to power your personalised career guidance experience within the platform. Aggregated, anonymised statistics may be used for academic research purposes at Egerton University.

Your conversations are stored privately in your account and are not used to train any AI model. Conversations are accessible only to you and to authorised platform administrators in exceptional circumstances (e.g. investigation of a reported safety concern).

Platform administrators with the Admin role can access aggregated, school-level analytics (e.g. how many students have completed the assessment, overall distribution of personality types). Individual student results are only visible in the PDF career report, which an admin can generate for a specific student — this is intended for use by school guidance counsellors in student support meetings.
